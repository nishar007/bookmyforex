How To Detect And Avoid Common Frauds

Online frauds and scams are at an all time high across India and currency exchange and international money transfers are often targeted by fraudsters. Read on to know how you can identify and avoid falling prey to these schemes

Transfer Money Now

Commonly Occurring Frauds

Imposter Scam - BookMyForex Staff

  • Beware of fraudsters pretending to be employees of BookMyForex. BookMyForex staff will never ask you to share your passwords, OTPs or any other sensitive info that might be used to access your account.

  • It is critical that you never share your account details with anyone, including close family members. Also, please double check the links sent to you as you might be on an offsite payment link, please double check that the link redirects you to www.bookmyforex.com

  • Further when expecting delivery of forex products at home, always ask your the delivery person to send a confirmation link to ensure that the correct product is being delivered to you

Emergency Scam

  • While in most imposter scams, fraudsters pretend to be BookMyForex staff, be wary of receiving seemingly genuine notifications/ calls from your friends/ family or co-workers, requesting you to make a payment urgently or to share any other sensitive information that would allow them to access your account or any external details

  • Please note it might be hackers trying to gain your trust by pretending to be close to the significant others and this enables scammers to create extremely genuine looking deep-fake voices and notifications

  • To ensure that you are being contacted by a genuine person, call them back on their number and ask them to mention some facts about you that only they will know

Fake Job Offer Scam

  • In this scam, the scammer pretends to be a foreign corporation or an employment agent and reaches out to let you know you have been selected for the job in overseas. Shortly afterwards, they will ask you to deposit payment to take care of a few expenses before you can start your employment

  • However, in case you choose to believe the claim, always double check the validity of the job by contacting the foreign corporation and performing necessary due diligence on the foreign employer

Rental Scam

  • Generally found in universities and cities where rentals are in short supply, these scams are orchestrated via listings that are too-good-to-be-true rental accommodations

  • Avoid deposit theft by making sAvoid deposit theft by making sure that you post unverified rental accommodations. Do proper due diligence including calling up the rental building management to confirm the listing ahead of making any payments

Lottery/ Inheritance/ Grant Scams

  • Scammers may reach out to you claiming that you have won a lottery or inheritance money. Soon afterwards they will ask you to send pay a deposit/ confirmation amount etc. to claim your reward/ money

  • Needless to say, such notifications should be unilaterally ignored and you should not respond to these scammers

Romance/ Friendship Scams

  • Met someone new online? If your connection requests payment to pay for any emergency or travel expenses, it is most likely a scam

  • Regardless of your emotional attachment, never make payments to people you have only met online and not met in-person
